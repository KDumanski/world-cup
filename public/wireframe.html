System

How the whole thing works

User flow

  1. Land on the country map — 16 pins across USA/Canada/Mexico.
  2. Click a city — map flies in; URL becomes /city/new-york.
  3. Filter — pick your nation/team, or “showing the match”, “open now”.
  4. Scan pins — colored by category, pulsing if showing a live game.
  5. Tap a spot — detail card: address, hours, which match it’s showing, directions.
  6. Go — directions hand off to Google/Apple Maps.

Filters (sidebar)

By nation / team By category 📺 Showing the match 🕐 Open now ⭐ Big gathering spots Distance to stadium

Data & tech layers

🗺️ Map — Mapbox GL JS

Dark style. GeoJSON layer of spots; cluster at country zoom, individual pins at city zoom; flyTo() on city select.

📊 Data — cities.js (already built)

Each spot gets coords, category, nation, hours, and a matchSchedule (which game it’s showing & when).

🎛️ Filter state

One filtered list drives both the sidebar list and the map pins — toggling a chip repaints instantly (client-side, no reload).

📺 “Showing the match”

Per-venue field tied to the official fixture list. A venue pulses gold when one of its broadcasts is live now.

🌍 Thin-diaspora teams

Some nations have few US spots — we surface the nearest big gathering + official fan zones, and say so honestly rather than show an empty map.

This wireframe is the plan. Next step would be wiring a real Mapbox map to the existing cities.js data, adding lat/lng, hours, and showingMatch to each spot.
